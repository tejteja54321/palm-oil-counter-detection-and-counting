About the Project.

The "Palm Oil Counter" project aims to leverage state-of-the-art deep learning models, specifically YOLOv10, for the detection and counting of palm oil fruits in plantations. By classifying the fruits into four categories: ripened, underripe, abnormal, and overripe, the model assists plantation workers and farm owners in making informed decisions about harvesting and plantation management. The project focuses on the deployment of a highly efficient deep learning model that can quickly and accurately identify these classes in real-time.

1 Ripened: This class represents the fully matured palm oil fruits that are ready for harvest. The YOLOv10 model detects these fruits based on specific color and texture features that are indicative of ripeness. Accurate detection of ripened fruits ensures timely harvesting and optimal oil production.

2 Underripe: The underripe class includes fruits that are not yet fully matured and require more time to develop. YOLOv10 helps plantation managers track the growth stages of the fruits, enabling better planning for harvest timings and reducing premature harvesting.

3 Abnormal: The abnormal class captures fruits that may have grown improperly, potentially due to pests, diseases, or environmental factors. Identifying abnormal fruits through YOLOv10 allows plantation managers to take corrective actions, such as pest control or fertilization, to maintain healthy crops.

4 Overripe: The overripe class contains fruits that have surpassed their ideal harvesting time and may start decaying. Detecting overripe fruits through YOLOv10 helps avoid loss due to spoilage, optimizing plantation output and reducing waste.
